About Me

I have my Bachelor's Degree in Electrical Engineering from the Georgia Institute of Technology and my Master's Degree in Computer Science from Clemson University.

I have had a varied career. I began my engineering career as a switching power supply engineer, transitioned to be an analog hardware designer, and then became a signal transformer designer.

I was the team leader of the group of hardware engineers who designed the Vectron Watt-Hour Meter.

I realized that gaining experience in a field means that, while you know more and more with time, your knowledge becomes more and more specialized until you know everything about nothing.

I decided to go to graduate school and learn about software engineering while I still knew more than nothing as a way to broaden my knowledge base.

Now, I'm learning about web page design to again broaden that knowledge base.
